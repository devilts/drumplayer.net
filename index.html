Snare Drum Description: The snare drum is smaller than the kick and has metal wires (called snares) stretched across the bottom head. This creates a sharp, crisp sound. Sound: Bright, sharp “crack” or “snap” with a bit of rattle from the snares. It cuts through the mix and often marks the backbeat. Common Use: Used for backbeats in rock, pop, and funk; rolls and accents in marching bands.

Tom-Toms (Toms)

Description: Medium-sized drums mounted above the kick or on stands. Usually come in a set of high, mid, and low toms.

Sound: Warm, resonant “dun” or “boom” tones, with pitch varying by drum size. Used for fills and rolls.

Common Use: Fills, rolls, and dynamic accents in rock, jazz, and other genres.
